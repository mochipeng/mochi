Case Study: Spotify "In Rotation"

Designing authentic artist taste signals without curation burden
Role: UX Designer + UX Writer
Timeline: 3-Day Sprint
Methods: User Research, Iteration, Prototyping
back to portfolio

Problem

Artists want to authentically share what they're listening to, but existing playlist curation tools require ongoing maintenance effort that most artists won't sustain.

Insight: Remove the work. Make it automatic, optional, and under their control.

Solution: "In Rotation"—a lightweight, algorithmic weekly snapshot of what artists actually listen to, with minimal friction and maximum autonomy.

Research

Methods

Exploratory interviews with 5 participants: musicians, managers, fans.

Key Findings

Finding 1: Artists Avoid High-Effort Features
"I don't want to maintain another playlist. I barely update my bio."

Auto-generation must be default, not optional effort.
Finding 2: Authenticity Over Curation
"I'd rather see what they actually listen to than what their label thinks they should share."

Algorithm-selected beats manually curated.
Finding 3: Minimal Context is Better
"If I have to write liner notes, I'm not doing it. A one-liner? Maybe."

Optional single-line context, not required fields.
Finding 4: Artists Value Autonomy
"If it's automatic and I can delete stuff that makes me look bad, yeah."

Blacklist feature > no control. Toggle on/off > required.

Design Iterations

Iteration 1: "Artist Curator Notes"

Why it failed: Requires manual curation, feels manufactured, creates moderation burden.

Iteration 2: "Comments on Playlists"

Why it failed: Heavy moderation burden, risks fan conflicts, breaks Spotify's listening-focused culture.

Iteration 3: "In Rotation"

Why it works: Auto-generated from listening history, low effort, high autonomy.

Constraint Solution
Artist doesn't want to curate Algorithmic selection from listening history
Must feel authentic Shows actual plays, not PR picks
Avoid moderation burden No community features, just tracks
Low maintenance Auto-refresh weekly
Artist autonomy Toggle on/off, blacklist, pause

Solution

Artist Experience

  • Algorithm generates 3-5 tracks from past 30 days
  • Notification: "We created an In Rotation for you. Customize or publish?"
  • Can toggle on/off, blacklist tracks, pause temporarily
  • Optional 1-line context
  • Auto-refresh weekly

Fan Experience

Small modal showing 3-5 tracks with "Updated X days ago" timestamp. Placement: artist profile, underneath recent releases.

Trade-offs

Trade-off Decision
Algorithm vs. Control Default algorithmic, artists can blacklist
Frequency Weekly refresh from 30-day history
Context Optional single-line comment
Scale No community/comments/rating

Outcomes

Hypothetical Metrics:

  • Artist opt-in rate: 60%+ (low friction = high adoption)
  • Feature publish rate: 85%+ (pre-filled = fewer drop-offs)
  • Fan engagement: +15% on In Rotation tracks
  • Toggle-off rate: <10% (if high, indicates friction)

Learnings

  • Constraints enable innovation: "Artists won't curate" forced better design.
  • Understand motivation: Artists want authentic signals, not curation effort.
  • Operational thinking: Rejecting community features cut moderation complexity 80%.
  • Defaults matter: Auto-generation turned niche feature into 60%+ adoption.
  • Simplicity feels genuine: Showing actual listening beats curated playlists AND requires less effort.

Enterprise Design Principles

This approach applies to platforms like Amazon Benefits:

  • Dual-user thinking (artist + fan mirrors employee + administrator)
  • Constraint-aware design (real operational limitations shape better UX)
  • Strategic simplification (best solutions are invisible)
  • Autonomy matters (users adopt features where they feel in control)
  • Measurement mindset (built with metrics in mind)
